#### Why did I learn programming by myself and become a programmer when I was 34 years old?

It is unusual that a middle-aged man like me decided to learn programming and web development, and became a software engineer at the age of 34. In June 2020, I had been learning for about a year in my spare time when I was doing short-term, unstable jobs near my hometown, and finally got a job as an intern developer in September 2021.

In an interview, the recruiter was confused and astounded when he knew that I was able to learn a programming language on my own in such an old age. Finally, he gave me an offer. The power motivating me to learn is that I want to find a stable job in or near my hometown so that I can stay with my family. 

I had been working in another big city far away from my home for many years, and it was difficult to settle in there for an ordinary person like me due to the ridiculously high price of property. Before I get married, I bought a flat in a county near my hometown; it is my home thereafter. 

My wife is a teacher at the school in my hometown. In China, teacher is one of the three stable and best professions, which are civil servant, doctor(or nurse), and teacher. She can’t change her job and doesn’t like living noisy big cities. So I tried going back to my hometown or nearby cities when I was a plastic injection mould designer. Unfortunately, there were a handful of vacancies for me. Before I started a job as a programmer, I had tried to stay with my family by doing various jobs, such as short-term ones. It was impossible to have a stable income by doing so; I had to find a sustainable way to earn money to support my family.

After searching for what jobs are in high demand in the city where I am living or nearby ones, I made a decision to learn a popular programming language, Java, and web development. There were two reasons: many IT companies and even manufacturing factories were recruiting Java developers; the other reason is that I’m a little bit interested in it. At first, I was trying to learn it by myself. Unexpectedly, I managed to learn a programming language and web development effectively without entering a vocational school when many younger graduates from universities pay high tuition fee to learn practical programming skills to qualify for a job.  Since all the programming language and documents are written in English, I realised that it was my English efficiency that made me an efficient learner, so that I was not deterred by the English warnings on the black CLI(command-line interface) when there are problems. Then I am able to tackle them by searching for solutions on the Internet. As a result, I got a job as a Java developer in a city near my home, and I could go back home every weekend; I was very happy at that time because before that, I could only go back home about every two months or longer. I had been doing it for over three years before I quit it to do an intensive English study for an IELTS test and apply for an on-campus program. 

#### Why do I build this website?

If I had enough time, I would build my website on GitHub pages. As a programmer, it is quite convenient to build a website based on a repository in GitHub. I always write essays, journals and so forth, which helps me improve English writing and will offer sentimental value when I'm old. 

 However, as far as I know, I should build it from scratch. There must be some templates somewhere. In order to save a middle-aged man's precious time, I will find them later.

Presumably, there are grammatical errors, misspelled words and inappropriate expression in these essays, but it is acknowledged that making mistakes is inevitable when you are learning a language. Whereas, that does not mean I will ignore that.
